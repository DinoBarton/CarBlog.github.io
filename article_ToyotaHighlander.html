The Toyota Highlander: A Versatile and Reliable Midsize SUV for Every Journey

Explore the reliability, practicality, and family-friendly features of the Toyota Highlander.

Nathan PidgelyJun 27 · 5 min. read
Share this

The Toyota Highlander, first introduced in 2000, has become a staple in the midsize SUV market, renowned for its reliability, practicality, and family-friendly features. The Highlander's design strikes a balance between rugged utility and sleek sophistication, making it appealing to a broad range of buyers. With its spacious interior, comfortable seating for up to eight passengers, and ample cargo space, the Highlander is perfectly suited for both daily commutes and long road trips.

Under the hood, the Highlander offers a variety of powertrains to meet different needs. The standard model comes equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine that delivers a robust 295 horsepower, ensuring smooth and responsive performance. For those seeking better fuel efficiency, Toyota offers a hybrid version that combines a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with electric motors, achieving impressive fuel economy without sacrificing power. The Highlander's all-wheel-drive option further enhances its capability, providing confidence and control in diverse driving conditions.

Safety and technology are key strengths of the Toyota Highlander. The SUV comes standard with Toyota Safety Sense 2.5+, a suite of advanced safety features including adaptive cruise control, lane departure warning, and automatic emergency braking. The Highlander's infotainment system is user-friendly, featuring an 8-inch touchscreen with Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and Amazon Alexa compatibility, ensuring seamless connectivity for all passengers.
